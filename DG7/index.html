Jump The Gun

  • Originality: The game is a platformer that aims to use the recoil from the player's weapon as a primary method of movement.
  • Technical Merit: The game uses a tilemap to build the level and directly translates the tiles there to tangible objects. Weapon pickups are now placed in the level with a tilemap as an object layer.
  • Prototype Postmortem: Again I tried to implement complex P2 physics and grappling functionality. This time I tried to use raycasting rather than a weapon to shoot the grappling hook at a platform. This caused an immense amount of trouble and never worked properly. Instead I refined the gun recoil movement idea so that the player would be pushed opposite to the direction they fired. This gave me a kind of twitch air movement to play with. I added some variance in bullet speed to make it a bit more interesting. Enemies have also been added to the game.

  • Gameplay: The player can use W/A/D to Jump/Move Left/Move Right. The spacebar will fire the player's weapon at the mouse and recoil the player in the opposite direction. This can be used as a form of movement but is planned to be limited by ammo in the future. Pressing X will switch weapon modes. The first, a shotgun, has limited range, high recoil, and is more influenced by gravity. The second is more of a sniper rifle with more range, faster bullets, less drop-off, and less recoil.
  • Strategies: Currently, the player can just spam fire and essentially have a jetpack to move around with. While movement around most of the level is possible with normal jumping, Spamming the shotgun or the machine gun may still be a more viable movement strategy.
